Who We Are

We are a group of researchers from the Senior High School Department at St. Mary’s Academy of Guagua, Inc., located in San Roque, Guagua, Pampanga. Our commitment is centered on developing innovative solutions that help communities become more prepared for natural disasters, particularly flooding, which remains a timely and significant concern in our country.

Our Mission

"To create a flood monitoring and early-warning system that enhances community preparedness and disaster response through timely warnings and important hotline information dissemination."

Critical Context: The Cebu Floods

Real-world events in November 2025 demonstrate why systems like WATCHEYE are non-negotiable for public safety.

Incident Report

Typhoon Kalmaegi (Tino) Floods

In November 2025, Typhoon Kalmaegi triggered catastrophic flash floods across the Philippines. Cebu province was devastated, with over 49 fatalities and 400,000 residents displaced.

Families were forced to roof-tops as water levels rose within minutes in areas like Mandaue City. WATCHEYE is designed to bridge this gap, providing the crucial minutes needed for evacuation.

Reference: Wright, G. (2025). "More than 85 dead as typhoon wreaks havoc in the Philippines." BBC News.

The Technology Stack

Precision hardware for rapid emergency response.

Central Hub

Built on Arduino Uno for robust sensor data processing and logical switching.

  • Fast detection cycles
  • Redundant logic paths

Precision Detection

Uses high-sensitivity Ultrasonic Sensors to measure water rise without physical contact.

  • Millimeter precision
  • Non-contact monitoring

Remote Alerts

Integrated with the GSM Module (SIM800L) for cellular notification broadcasting.

  • SMS Hotline info
  • Global network support

Visual & Audible Warning System

GREEN ALERT

Safe water levels detected. LED turns on.

ORANGE ALERT

Rise detected. Warning LED turns on.

RED ALERT

Critical level. LED turns on and buzzer activates for immediate alarm.

Process & Results

The study employed a quantitative developmental research design. We built and tested the system to ensure reliability under simulated flood conditions.

01

Detection Integrity

The HC-SR04 sensor showed high accuracy in correlating ultrasonic bounce time with water surface distance.

02

SMS Response

The SIM800L module successfully delivered SMS notifications within 10-15 seconds of threshold breach.

03

LED Warning Logic

Verified sequential lighting of Green, Orange, and Red indicators corresponding to precise water-level increments.

04

Audible Alert Activation

Confirmed that reaching the Red critical threshold successfully triggers the buzzer to buzz, providing an immediate audible alarm alongside the visual signal.

"The synchronized LED and buzzer system serves as a multi-sensory early-warning tool for enhanced community safety."
